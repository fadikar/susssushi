What We Make

Korean Sushi — A Roll of Tradition

Korean sushi, known as kimbap (김밥), is a beloved Korean street food made by rolling seasoned rice, vegetables, and protein fillings in sheets of dried seaweed (gim), then slicing the roll into bite-sized pieces.

While it may look similar to Japanese maki sushi, kimbap has its own distinct identity. The rice is seasoned with sesame oil and salt — not vinegar — giving it a nutty, fragrant flavour. Fillings are typically cooked or pickled, rather than raw, making kimbap a hearty, satisfying meal on the go.

At Sus's Sushi, we honour this tradition while adding our own home-kitchen touch — using fresh local ingredients and carefully balanced flavours to create rolls you'll want to order again and again.

🌍  Origin: Korea — a staple of Korean lunch culture since the early 20th century

Inside Every Roll

Core Kimbap Ingredients

Traditional Korean kimbap combines seasoned rice, roasted seaweed, and balanced fillings for flavour, texture, and nutrition in every bite.

Gim (Roasted Seaweed)

Thin roasted seaweed sheets form the outer wrap. Gim adds umami depth and keeps the roll tightly structured.

Short-Grain Rice

Kimbap uses warm short-grain rice, lightly seasoned with salt and sesame oil for a nutty, savory finish.

Toasted Sesame Oil

A key flavor note in Korean rolls. It is mixed into the rice and brushed lightly to add aroma and richness.

Cucumber

Fresh cucumber adds crisp texture and clean flavor, helping balance richer fillings like chicken, paneer, and shrimp.

Vegetable Fillings

Common fillings include carrot, cucumber, spinach, and burdock root for crunch, freshness, and color contrast.

Protein Layer

Classic kimbap uses egg strips, fish cake, tuna, beef, or crab stick. Your menu variants feature chicken, paneer, and shrimp.
